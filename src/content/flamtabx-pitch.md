# FlamTabX

A climate adaptive, bio based coating system that makes homes and workplaces **cooler, drier, and healthier** by cutting heat gain, managing moisture, and resisting mold, without assuming everyone can buy more air conditioning.

> **At a glance:** We combine passive thermal behavior, breathable bio based film, and antimicrobial function in one envelope layer, aimed first at hot, humid India and MSME workshops where heat is a productivity and health issue, not only a comfort preference.

---

## The problem

Extreme heat is now a **climate, public health, and labor** issue, not a seasonal inconvenience. Indian summers routinely cross **45–50°C**; indoor conditions in tin and concrete homes often sit near **38–40°C** for long stretches, including at night. Humidity compounds the story: damp walls, mold, and poor air quality spike in monsoon seasons.

**Access to cooling is deeply unequal.** Only a small fraction of households run air conditioning; the rest depend on fans, shading, and endurance, while grids strain under rising cooling demand and **carbon intensive** power mixes.

**The gap:** Heat action plans emphasize emergency response; building-level, **low energy** interventions that also address **moisture and indoor health** at scale are still scarce.

### Key figures

| Signal | Order of magnitude |
|--------|-------------------|
| Heat exposure & mortality | Heat-related deaths run to thousands annually in India; global projections rise sharply toward mid-century without adaptation. |
| Productivity | Heat stress could cost India on the order of **~5.8% of working hours** by 2030: millions of full time equivalent jobs. |
| Indoor experience | Studies in warm, humid India report prolonged hours above **35°C** indoors; most people report heat symptoms (fatigue, headaches, dehydration). |

## Why the status quo falls short

Today’s market is a **stack of single-purpose products**:

- **Reflective / cool roof coatings** (major paint brands and specialists) lower surface temperature but often **do not** fully solve indoor comfort, night time heat, or wall moisture.
- **Insulation systems** (Sika, Saint-Gobain, etc.) work for new or high-end retrofits: **expensive** and awkward for informal housing and thin budgets.
- **Air conditioning** cools effectively but raises **bills, peak load, and emissions**, out of reach for most households.
- **Waterproofing** addresses leaks but can **trap** humidity if not paired with breathable, climate-aware design.
- **Antimicrobial paints** often **fade** under combined heat + moisture stress.

**Radiative cooling R&D** is exciting but still constrained by **cost, durability, and deployment** for mass markets.

> **The insight:** Buyers patch heat, moisture, and hygiene separately. FlamTabX targets a **single affordable passive layer** that addresses all three for roofs and walls.

## Who we serve

**Primary (residential, India, hot humid):** Middle- and lower-middle-income households in independent homes, low-rise housing, and informal construction, where indoor heat is severe and **AC penetration is still low**, while cooling demand is rising fast.

**Secondary (MSMEs):** Workshops, warehouses, and light manufacturing where **indoor heat hits productivity and safety**. These operators need **low capex** interventions that do not assume new HVAC.

## The product

FlamTabX is a **climate adaptive, bio based coating** for roofs and walls that aims to:

- **Reduce heat ingress** via reflectivity, UV protection, and **phase change style** thermal buffering (smoothing peaks).
- **Manage moisture** through a **breathable** matrix, reducing damp walls and mold risk.
- **Improve surface hygiene** with antimicrobial design suited to **humid** operation.
- Explore **stimuli responsive** behavior so performance tracks temperature and humidity.

**Outcome for users:** cooler-feeling interiors, better sleep and focus, lower pressure to run AC, and healthier air adjacent to treated surfaces, applied with **familiar painting workflows**, not exotic site infrastructure.

## Traction and validation

**Challenges we own:** affordability at scale, user education (passive vs AC), and distribution into real job sites. Mitigations: **local bio based inputs**, standard application methods, and **visible pilot outcomes** (comfort + savings narratives).

**Early market signal:** Conversations and demos with MSMEs; **two Letters of Intent** for industrial pilots: **GreenCircle Chemicals (Mumbai)** and **Sargenic Chemical Pvt. Ltd.** Residential society discussions are active in parallel.

**Prototype status:** Lab and panel work on metal and concrete; **adhesion, scratch, flexibility, drying, gloss, water uptake, solvent resistance, and antimicrobial** screens underway. Industrial sheet tests showed **meaningful surface temperature reduction** vs. untreated metal under exposure. Field pilots are the next instrumentation step.

## Go-to-market and economics

**Phase 1 (pilot first):** Deploy with high heat MSMEs and selected residential sites; instrument temperature, comfort feedback, and energy proxies; refine formulation and application SOPs.

**Phase 2 (scale via channels):** Contractors, industrial clusters, facility managers, repeat maintenance coats, then systematic residential rollout.

**What we track**

- **Impact:** kWh avoided and CO₂ avoided per treated building (see also our [passive cooling calculator](/tools/energy-savings) and [methodology write-up](/blog/energy-calculator)).
- **Product:** Δ surface / indoor temperature vs. controls, durability in monsoon cycles.
- **Business:** pilot count, paid conversion, repeat orders.

**Revenue model:** Per **m² / per project** coating sales; optional **application** via partners; maintenance and **B2B bulk** over time.
